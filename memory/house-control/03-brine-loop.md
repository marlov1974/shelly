# Brine Loop

## Role

The brine loop is used as the cold source for free cooling through a heat exchanger into the floor loop.

## Known design facts

- Brine temperature can be around 6 C.
- The brine side should be treated as a low-temperature source with condensation implications downstream.
- Ethanol-based brine spill previously caused VOC sensor elevation for several days, which is plausible because ethanol is volatile and can remain in surfaces/air for an extended period.

## Pump placement principle

The larger/stronger pump is generally better suited to the brine side if the brine loop has higher pressure drop or if higher primary-side flow is needed to transfer heat through the exchanger. The floor side should be matched to floor-loop hydraulics and comfort constraints.

## Open design questions

- Required brine flow for a given cooling power.
- Practical heat exchanger capacity.
- Maximum safe cooling power before floor condensation risk dominates.
- Whether brine flow should be controlled directly or simply enabled with fixed pump curve.
