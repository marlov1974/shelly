# Heat Pumps

## Current observations

The heat pumps can maintain floor-heating supply around 30 C with continuous low-load operation. User observations indicate that pump/compressor behavior at very low percentage operation should be evaluated carefully; a stable low percentage may be efficient, but can also represent near-idle behavior depending on compressor/control design.

## Operating hypotheses

Potential strategies discussed:

- Continuous lower-temperature operation, e.g. one pump running steadily around low load.
- Time-window operation, e.g. resting during selected periods and compensating by running harder.
- Compensation by raising supply temperature rather than only changing water flow.
- Coordination with electricity price and thermal inertia may be useful later.

## Control questions to keep open

- Can flow be directly configured/owned in the heat pump menus?
- What is the practical minimum efficient compressor load?
- Is it better to increase flow, supply temperature, or runtime when compensating for rest periods?
- How should heat pump behavior coordinate with FTX heat/cool batteries?

## Wi-Fi notes

Heat pump Wi-Fi adapters may have difficulty joining modern/high-tech Wi-Fi. A stable 2.4 GHz network with conservative settings is preferred for technical devices.
