# Floor Heating and Cooling

## Role

The floor loop is used for normal heating and potential free cooling. Cooling through floors must be limited by dew point and comfort.

## Current known values

Observed/discussed values include:

- Floor-loop return around 30 C before cooling.
- Desired cooling supply may be as low as about 17 C in worst case, but this must be constrained by dew point and floor condensation risk.
- Flow readings can be coarse when displayed with only one decimal m3/h.

## Cooling safety

Floor cooling must not be controlled only by desired room temperature. It needs at least:

- indoor air temperature
- indoor relative humidity
- calculated dew point
- minimum floor/supply temperature margin above dew point

## Practical principle

Prefer conservative cooling. The floor system has large thermal inertia and condensation risk. It is better to undercool than to create wet floors or hidden moisture risk.
